Component 3 — Tip / Gauge Card
💡
Gauge & Tension Gauge isn't critical for size accuracy, but a tight, consistent tension is non-negotiable. If you can see daylight between your stitches, drop to a 1.75 mm hook. Stuffing peeking through flesh-tone yarn is the fastest way to make an amigurumi look unfinished.
Component 4 — Phase Section + Round Blocks

Instructions

Phase 1: The Arms

The arms are the smallest piece in the project — finished, each one is about an inch and a half long with a flesh-tone hand at the bottom, a blue sleeve, and a tiny white scalloped ruffle marking the transition between the two. They look like miniature ice cream cones with a frilled collar. The key move is Round 5: working in back loops only frees up the front loops for that scalloped sleeve cuff later. Make both, leave both with live yarn attached, and set them aside — you'll fuse them directly into the body in Phase 2.

Make 2. Begin with Flesh yarn.

Round 1: 6 SC in a MR (6)
Rounds 2–4: SC in each st around (6) Switch to Blue yarn.
Round 5: SC in BLO of each st around (6) (You're skipping the front loops on purpose — they stay free for the ruffle in step 5. The arm will feel slightly stiffer at this round because of the loop change. That's correct.)
Rounds 6–9: SC in each st around (6) Do not fasten off the main arm yarn. Secure the live loop with a stitch marker.
Sleeve Ruffle (worked into the free front loops of Round 5): Join a separate strand of Blue yarn into any front loop of Rnd 5. [Ch 3, skip 1 st, sl st into next st] × 3 around.
FO the ruffle strand only and weave in those ends.
!
Don't Cut the Main Arm Yarn

Only the ruffle strand fastens off. The main arm yarn stays live with the stitch marker holding it. Both arms need to be ready to attach when you hit Round 15 of the body — if you cut the yarn, you can't do the inline join.

A simple scarf is the perfect first project for new crocheters. You will practice your basic stitches while creating something beautiful and useful!

## Materials Needed

- **Yarn**: 2 skeins worsted weight yarn (approximately 400 yards)
- **Hook**: 5.5mm (I) crochet hook
- **Notions**: Yarn needle, scissors

## Finished Size

Approximately 6 inches wide x 60 inches long

## Gauge

14 hdc = 4 inches (gauge is not critical for this project)

## Skills You Will Learn

- Chain stitch
- Half double crochet
- Turning chains
- Weaving in ends

## Pattern Instructions

### Foundation

Ch 22.

### Row 1

Hdc in 3rd ch from hook (skipped chains count as first hdc), hdc in each ch across. (20 hdc)

### Row 2

Ch 2 (counts as hdc), turn, hdc in each st across.

### Rows 3-150

Repeat Row 2 until scarf reaches desired length or you run out of yarn.

### Finishing

Fasten off and weave in all ends.

## Optional Fringe

1. Cut 40 pieces of yarn, each 12 inches long
2. Fold 2 pieces in half
3. Insert hook through edge of scarf
4. Pull folded loop through
5. Pull ends through loop and tighten
6. Repeat across both short ends

## Tips for Beginners

- **Count your stitches** at the end of each row
- **Use stitch markers** to mark the first stitch
- **Take breaks** to avoid hand fatigue
- **Do not worry about mistakes** - they are part of learning!

Congratulations on completing your first crochet project!

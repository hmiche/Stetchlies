Choosing the right yarn can make or break your crochet project. With so many options available, it is easy to feel overwhelmed. This guide will help you understand yarn types and make confident choices.

## Understanding Yarn Weights

Yarn weight refers to the thickness of the yarn strand. The Craft Yarn Council has standardized yarn weights into categories:

### Weight Categories

1. **Lace (0)** - Very fine, used for delicate doilies and shawls
2. **Fingering (1)** - Thin yarn for socks and lightweight garments
3. **Sport (2)** - Light yarn for baby items and light sweaters
4. **DK (3)** - Medium-light, versatile for many projects
5. **Worsted (4)** - Medium weight, most popular for general crochet
6. **Bulky (5)** - Thick yarn for quick, cozy projects
7. **Super Bulky (6)** - Very thick, makes chunky blankets fast

## Fiber Types

### Natural Fibers

- **Cotton** - Cool, breathable, great for summer items and dishcloths
- **Wool** - Warm, elastic, perfect for winter garments
- **Alpaca** - Luxuriously soft, warmer than wool
- **Silk** - Lustrous drape, often blended with other fibers

### Synthetic Fibers

- **Acrylic** - Affordable, easy care, machine washable
- **Polyester** - Durable, holds color well
- **Nylon** - Strong, often added for durability

## Matching Yarn to Projects

- **Blankets**: Worsted or bulky acrylic for easy washing
- **Amigurumi**: Cotton or acrylic in worsted weight
- **Garments**: Natural fibers for breathability
- **Baby items**: Soft, washable yarns like baby acrylic

Choose your yarn wisely, and your projects will turn out beautifully!
